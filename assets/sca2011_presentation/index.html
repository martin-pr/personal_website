introduction

footsliding

  • very common animation artifact
    • present in almost all marketed VR applications and games
  • many different causes
    • motion capture processing artifacts
    • motion editing
    • motion blending
    • retargeting
    • character responsiveness constraints
  • but the visible result is always similar:
    foot movement inconsistent with the expected foot-ground interactions

footskate cleanup experiment

motivation and aim

  • now we know that the perceptual thresholds for footsliding are quite low
  • shall we correct the footsliding? Or will the correction make the motion quality even worse?
  • and if we choose to correct, how?

footskate cleanup experiment

cleanup methods

  • not many methods in literature, we choose to use two:
  • method introduced by Kovar et al., 2002:
    • comprehensive method, state-of-the-art
    • root displacement, smoothing, leg IK, leg lengthening
  • lengthening only
    • significantly simpler (last step of the previous method only)
    • based on Harrison et al - limb lengthening up to 20\% might go unnoticed

footskate cleanup experiment

cleanup methods

  • not many methods in literature, we choose to use two:
  • method introduced by Kovar et al., 2002:
    • comprehensive method, state-of-the-art
    • root displacement, smoothing, leg IK, leg lengthening
  • lengthening only
    • significantly simpler (last step of the previous method only)
    • based on Harrison et al - limb lengthening up to 20\% might go unnoticed
  • we will refer to these methods as:
    • U - uncorrected
    • K - Kovar's correction method
    • L - lengthening correction

conclusions and future work

  • even very small levels of footsliding are perceivable
    • especially when environment cues are provided
  • correcting is always preferred to not correcting
  • of the two tested method, the simpler limb lengthening method is preferred
  • future work:
    • test more realistic characters
    • test general limb-lengthening saliency on humanoid characters
    • test effects of camera angle and motion
    • test effects of user's attention
    • different motions than walking

baseline experiment

experiment procedure

  • adaptive staircase procedure, yes/no variant
    • designed according to Garcia-Perez: Yes-no staircases with fixed step sizes: psychometric properties and optimal setup, 2001
    • double staircase (+ two variants running at the same time)
    • fixed up and down steps
    • down/up step ratio of 0.871
    • convergence at 52.38%
    • stopping condition at 20 reversals
